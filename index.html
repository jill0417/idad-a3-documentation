Guiding Values And Embodiment In The UI

The core guiding value of Emotion Dice is to create playful and reflective digital experiences through accessibility. Music visualises the unpredictability of emotions, paired with the animation to create a more sensory-rich interaction, and also leverages physical movements. Additionally, using this randomness not only as a game mechanic but also as a metaphor for the volatile nature of emotion supports the cognitive action. The experience supports diverse users through eye-catching and visually distinct emotion labels, ensuring clarity of purpose and inclusiveness. While expressive typography, soft gradients, and sound combine to transform a simple roll of the dice into a small act of emotional self-discovery.

Design Rationale

In my prototype, I used multisensory feedback to guide user interaction. When the user clicks a button, the dice image animates as it rolls, accompanied by a dice rolling sound and the change of the background colour. The emotional title and its matching soundtrack appear prominently, the music controls group logically below the song title, making the information design clean and letting the users feel the result intuitively. The action of rolling the dice is consistent with the triggered emotions, and the emotional atmosphere echoes the background colour and music, which strengthens the mapping. For learnability, there is only one primary button needed to interact, which has no unnecessary controls at first glance, making the prototype approachable for first-time users.

Significant Changes

After receiving the feedback, I implemented some key improvements. One of the major changes was creating a fair roll system, which ensures that every emotion face is shown up without immediate repeats with a random order for two cycles. After the 2 cycles, normal random rolling resumes. This can prevent repetition and make sure that they experience all the emotions, to make the whole exploration more engaging. I also added the background colour transition that tied to each emotion and added a dice rolling sound effect to improve the immersive and realistic experience. These changes not only improved UI usability but also deepened the conceptual consistency of emotional change and feedback design.

Production Timeline & Time Management

I first designed the dice-rolling logic and the concept of matching emotions, then made a preliminary draft of the basic UI. Next, I laid out the overall framework using HTML and focused on functionality with JavaScript, establishing dice logic and associating emotions with sounds. Once functionality is built, I utilise CSS to design the UI and appearance of the prototype, and I even drew my own icons for the dice. Based on user feedback, I first optimised the dice rolling loop system, added a non-repeating emotion loop to enhance usability, and then introduced background colour logic and tested the audio feedback. I also adjusted some elements of the UI, like instead of using the built-in audio player, I customised the buttons to better align with the style of the website and added an alert to warn of the sound output and make the website more accessible and clearer. In this project, I spent most of my time debugging the dice rolling queue logic and figuring out how to let it connect to the audio. I also considered adding some other functions like tone control and rolling history, but I prioritise completing and refining the main functions.

Most Challenging Design Aspect

The most challenging aspect of this project was implementing the fair roll system, which I had to have each emotion face show up once in a random order for two full cycles, then switch to normal random behaviour. At first, I didn't understand the logic behind this system and was confused about how to prevent duplicates in the scrolling queue and ensure that each expression appeared exactly once within a cycle. But through the one-on-one guidance of the teacher, I was able to break down the problem, gradually understand the logic, and slowly build a system through continuous research and experimentation, which gives me a sense of satisfaction and accomplishment.
